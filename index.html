All low-voltage gear lives in one wall-mounted cabinet on a plywood backer in the garage. This page shows the layout, how the cabinet attaches to the plywood and the plywood to the wall, and what equipment goes in which rack unit. Every item links to its product page.

Install summary

One 4'×4' × 3/4" plywood backer, sealed and painted to match wall. 5/16" × 3" lag bolts through plywood into each stud — 2 per stud, top and bottom (8 lags total). Bottom edge of plywood at 12" AFF; bottom of cabinet at 48" AFF.

What Ivan owns (electrical)

  • Dedicated 15A circuit for the rack — single NEMA 5-15R duplex receptacle directly under the cabinet, not shared with any other garage outlets. (Rack worst-case draw is ~1.5A — 15A is plenty; CyberPower PDU has a 5-15P plug, so 5-15R is the matching receptacle. Reason for "dedicated": no nuisance trips from shop tools / garage door opener sharing the circuit.)
  • LV stub-up: 1× 1.5" ENT (smurf tube) from this location to the attic, for all house CAT6 home runs to land at the patch panel.
  • Sonic fiber entry: separate 3/4" conduit from the exterior demarc to the bottom of the cabinet for the ONT fiber jumper.
  • Bonding: #6 ground from the cabinet ground lug (back of cabinet, see next diagram) to nearest panel ground.
  • No shared neutrals on the rack circuit.

What's pre-purchased — do not buy

  • NavePoint 6U cabinet, 1U vented shelf, CyberPower PDU, TRENDnet patch panel, UDM-SE, WD Purple 8TB, U-POE-at injector, (3) U7 Pro XG access points.
  • Ray supplies all in-wall CAT6 cable and patch-panel keystones. Short 1-ft patch cables (patch-panel-to-UDM) come with the UniFi order — do not buy. Ivan home-runs CAT6 from each AP / wall jack to this cabinet — leave 10' of slack at the rack end.

Back of cabinet — how it mounts to the plywood

Rear view — mounting screws into plywood

NavePoint Performance Series uses a hanger-bar mount (per the cabinet's owner's manual): the supplied hanger bar (part #9) is anchored to the wall first with 4 screws; the cabinet then drops onto two outward-facing hooks via two notches on its rear and is locked in place with 2 M4 screws. We anchor the hanger bar to the plywood, and the plywood to the studs with 8 lags. No cabinet screws land in studs directly — the hanger bar carries the load.

Patching — patch panel to UDM-SE

Short 1-ft Cat6 patch cables (included in the UniFi order US5474906, not separately purchased) jump from each keystone on the patch panel up to a PoE+ port on the UDM-SE. One cable per device. Label both ends with the destination room.

Six cables shown; up to all 24 patch ports usable. Each patch cable plugs into the keystone on the front of the patch panel, swoops up past the U4 blank, and lands on a UDM-SE PoE+ port. The 3rd AP run takes a detour through the U-POE-at injector sitting on the U6 shelf because the U7 Pro XG can pull more wattage than the UDM-SE PoE budget delivers on a heavily loaded port.

Wire-up flow — for Ivan

  1. Sonic fiber comes up through the dedicated bottom conduit → ONT (sits on the U5 shelf). ONT Ethernet out drops 2U down to the UDM-SE WAN port (U3).
  2. Each house CAT6 home run enters the cabinet through the bottom knockouts and is punched down on the U1 patch panel keystone directly above. Label every port (room + position).
  3. Short 1-ft patch cables (from the UniFi order — no need to buy) jump from U1 patch panel ports up past the U2 PDU to UDM-SE PoE+ ports (U3). Route along the right rail with velcro so they skirt the PDU face cleanly.
  4. All AC: every device's power cord plugs into the U2 PDU. PDU plugs into the dedicated 15A wall receptacle below the cabinet.
  5. 3rd AP (longest run): U1 patch panel port → U-POE-at injector (sitting on the U5 shelf) data-in, injector data+power-out → that AP. Injector wall-wart plugs into the U2 PDU.
