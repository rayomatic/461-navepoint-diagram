All low-voltage gear lives in one wall-mounted cabinet on a plywood backer in the garage. This page shows the layout, how the cabinet attaches to the plywood and the plywood to the wall, and what equipment goes in which rack unit. Every item links to its product page.

Install summary

One 4'×4' × 3/4" plywood backer, sealed and painted to match wall. 5/16" × 3" lag bolts through plywood into each stud — 2 per stud, top and bottom (8 lags total). Bottom edge of plywood at 12" AFF; bottom of cabinet at 48" AFF.

What Ivan owns (electrical)

  • Dedicated 20A circuit for the rack — single duplex receptacle directly under the cabinet, not shared with any other garage outlets.
  • LV stub-up: 1× 1.5" ENT (smurf tube) from this location to the attic, for all house CAT6 home runs to land at the patch panel.
  • Sonic fiber entry: separate 3/4" conduit from the exterior demarc to the bottom of the cabinet for the ONT fiber jumper.
  • Bonding: #6 ground from the cabinet ground lug (back of cabinet, see next diagram) to nearest panel ground.
  • No shared neutrals on the rack circuit.

What's pre-purchased — do not buy

  • NavePoint 6U cabinet, 1U vented shelf, CyberPower PDU, TRENDnet patch panel, UDM-SE, WD Purple 8TB, U-POE-at injector, (3) U7 Pro XG access points.
  • Ray supplies all CAT6 cable, keystones, and patch cables. Ivan home-runs CAT6 from each AP / wall jack to this cabinet — leave 10' of slack at the rack end, coiled at U2.

Back of cabinet — how it mounts to the plywood

Rear view — mounting screws into plywood

4 screws hold the cabinet to the plywood — corners of the rear plate. Plus the keyhole hanger at top-center for initial hang. 8 lag bolts hold the plywood to the studs. All cabinet screws land in plywood — they do not need to hit studs.

Stack, left to right: stud · drywall · plywood · cabinet rear · interior · glass door. Cabinet hangs entirely off the plywood (not into studs). Drywall stays continuous behind the plywood.

Rack elevation — what goes in each U

Patching — patch panel to UDM-SE

Short Cat6 patch cables (Ray supplies, 1–2 ft) jump from the keystone on the patch panel up to a PoE+ port on the UDM-SE. One cable per device. Label both ends with the destination room.

Six cables shown; up to all 24 patch ports usable. Each patch cable plugs into the keystone on the front of the patch panel, swoops up past the U4 blank, and lands on a UDM-SE PoE+ port. The 3rd AP run takes a detour through the U-POE-at injector sitting on the U6 shelf because the U7 Pro XG can pull more wattage than the UDM-SE PoE budget delivers on a heavily loaded port.

Wire-up flow — for Ivan

  1. Sonic fiber → ONT (sits on the U6 shelf). ONT Ethernet out → UDM-SE WAN port.
  2. Each house CAT6 run → punched down on U3 patch panel keystone. Label every port (room + position).
  3. Short patch cables (Ray supplies) from patch panel ports up to UDM-SE PoE+ ports for APs and cameras.
  4. All AC: every device's power cord plugs into the U1 PDU. PDU plugs into the dedicated 20A wall receptacle.
  5. 3rd AP (longest run): patch panel port → U-POE-at injector data-in, injector data+power-out → that AP. Injector wall-wart plugs into the PDU.
